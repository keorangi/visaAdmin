The terms below come up across this site. The definitions are short, plain-language readings of how Immigration New Zealand and MBIE use each one. The link after each definition goes to the primary source — that's the version that holds up at audit, not this page.

If a term you're chasing isn't here, the [INZ Operational Manual](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/opsmanual/) is the most complete source.

## Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV)

The work visa pathway that requires the employer — not the migrant — to hold accreditation with Immigration New Zealand before hiring. Most employer-facing obligations on this site sit under the AEWV framework.

Source: [Immigration New Zealand — AEWV](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants/employ-a-candidate/types-of-visa/accredited-employer-work-visa).

## Accredited employer

A New Zealand employer that has been granted accreditation by Immigration New Zealand to hire migrant workers under the AEWV. Accreditation comes with ongoing conditions, record-keeping obligations, and renewal triggers — it's not a one-time approval.

Source: [INZ — Employer accreditation](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants/become-accredited-employer).

## Post-Accreditation Check

The compliance check Immigration New Zealand runs after accreditation has been granted, to confirm the employer is meeting the conditions they accredited under. A failed check can produce an infringement notice, the loss of accreditation, or placement on the Stand-down List.

Source: [INZ — Maintaining your accreditation](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants/maintain-accreditation).

## National Occupation List (NOL)

The New Zealand–specific occupation classification rolling out across the 2026 settings, replacing or supplementing ANZSCO for AEWV purposes. The NOL determines skill level, wage threshold reasoning, Job Check evidence, and work-to-residence eligibility for each role.

Sources: [Stats NZ](https://www.stats.govt.nz/) for the classification methodology; [MBIE](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/) for the policy rationale; [Immigration New Zealand](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants) for how the NOL is applied to a particular visa.

Related field notes: [The 2026 National Occupation List — what's actually changing for accredited employers]({{< ref "/blog/national-occupation-list-2026" >}}).

## ANZSCO

The Australian and New Zealand Standard Classification of Occupations — the joint classification New Zealand has used for AEWV decisions until the NOL rollout. Older Job Checks and accreditation records will reference ANZSCO codes; new ones will increasingly reference NOL codes.

Source: [Stats NZ — ANZSCO](https://www.stats.govt.nz/).

## Job Check

The step in the AEWV process where Immigration New Zealand confirms the role being offered to a migrant meets the labour market test, pay threshold, and occupation classification rules. The Job Check is role-by-role, not employer-by-employer.

Source: [INZ — Job Check](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants/employ-a-candidate/job-check).

## Labour market test

The requirement to advertise a role to New Zealanders, and to engage with the Ministry of Social Development for some roles, before the role can be filled by a migrant under the AEWV. The labour market test is part of the Job Check.

Source: [INZ — Labour market test](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants/employ-a-candidate/job-check) and [Employment New Zealand](https://www.employment.govt.nz/).

## MSD engagement

The required engagement with the Ministry of Social Development — practically, with Work and Income — for certain roles before the labour market test is considered satisfied. The record needs to show date, role, outcome, and evidence.

Source: [Work and Income — Job Connect for employers](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/employers/).

## Median wage threshold

The wage figure published by Stats NZ that AEWV pay-band reasoning anchors against. The threshold is updated periodically; a role that was compliant at one threshold can drift out of compliance when the figure moves.

Source: [Stats NZ — Quarterly Employment Survey](https://www.stats.govt.nz/) and [INZ — AEWV wage requirements](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants/employ-a-candidate/types-of-visa/accredited-employer-work-visa).

## High-paying threshold

A higher wage threshold above the median wage, which determines eligibility for some AEWV pathways and reduces certain Job Check requirements. Where it applies, it must be evidenced — not assumed.

Source: [INZ — AEWV wage requirements](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants/employ-a-candidate/types-of-visa/accredited-employer-work-visa).

## Settlement information

The information accredited employers must give migrant workers at the start of employment, covering employment rights, life in New Zealand, and where to seek help. Delivery has to be timestamped — and the record has to show the worker received it within the required window.

Sources: [INZ — Settlement information](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants); [Employment New Zealand — Migrant workers](https://www.employment.govt.nz/starting-employment/who-is-an-employee/migrant-workers/).

## Stand-down List

The publicly published list of employers who have lost accreditation or otherwise been barred from hiring under the AEWV for a period. Placement on the list is a material business event — it stops migrant hiring and signals risk to existing visa holders, customers, and counterparties.

Source: [INZ — Stand-down list](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants).

## Infringement notice

A formal notice issued by Immigration New Zealand for a breach of AEWV conditions or related employer obligations. Notices carry a financial penalty and are recorded against the employer; multiple notices can lead to loss of accreditation.

Source: [INZ — Compliance and enforcement](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/employ-migrants).

## INZ Operational Manual

The full set of instructions immigration officers apply when assessing an AEWV decision. Where this site or any other summary appears to conflict with the manual, the manual wins. Most of what employers find on `immigration.govt.nz` is a plain-language summary of an underlying manual instruction.

Source: [INZ Operational Manual](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/opsmanual/).

## Worker Protection Act

Shorthand for the Worker Protection (Migrant and Other Employees) Act 2023, which broadened the obligations on employers to act on credible reports of migrant exploitation involving their own workplace. Relevant to AEWV employers because exploitation findings carry accreditation consequences.

Source: [legislation.govt.nz — Worker Protection (Migrant and Other Employees) Amendment Act 2023](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/).
