From 1 June 2026, anyone applying for an Accredited Employer Work Visa in a skill level 3 role must demonstrate a minimum standard of English. The requirement previously applied to skill levels 4 and 5 only. A job check token already in hand does not exempt an applicant from the new standard. If you are recruiting into level 3 roles, or have workers whose AEWVs come up for renewal after December 2026, the timing matters.

## Why this matters now

The change took effect two weeks ago. AEWV applications for skill level 3 occupations submitted from 1 June 2026 are subject to the new requirement.[^1] Skill level 3 spans a wide range of roles across sectors with large AEWV workforces — construction, hospitality, and care among them. In March 2026, INZ recognised 47 additional occupations across skill levels 1 to 3 under the National Occupation List, which increased the overall number of roles at this level.[^3] Employers who were not tracking this change may already have candidates in the pipeline for whom English evidence has not been gathered.

## What the standard requires and how applicants can meet it

There are three routes to meeting the requirement. Applicants who cannot use one of the experience-based options will need a qualifying test result before applying for a visa.

**Recognised language tests.** INZ sets a minimum score for each accepted test:[^2]

| Test | Minimum |
|------|---------|
| IELTS Academic or General Training | Overall 4.0 |
| TOEFL iBT | Overall 31 |
| PTE Academic | Overall 29 |
| Cambridge B2 First or B2 First for Schools | Overall 142 |
| OET | Grade D or higher in all four skills |

**Citizenship and work or study history.** Citizens of Canada, Ireland, the United Kingdom, or the United States who have spent five or more years working or studying in those countries, in Australia, or in New Zealand meet the requirement without a test.[^2]

**Qualifications.** Holding a qualification comparable to a New Zealand level 7 bachelor's degree, obtained from an approved country with at least two years' residence in that country during study, also satisfies the requirement. A level 8 or above postgraduate qualification from an approved country, with at least one year's residence during study, is an alternative.[^2]

## The exemptions that apply this year

Three groups are not affected by the 1 June 2026 change.

**Workers renewing before December 2026.** An applicant whose current AEWV expires on or before 1 December 2026, who applies for a further AEWV at skill level 3, is exempt from the new requirement. This transitional window covers workers already in the country whose renewal falls before the end of this year.[^1]

**Workers who already provided evidence.** If an applicant demonstrated English as part of a previous AEWV application, they are not required to provide it again.[^1]

**Specific visa types.** The requirement does not apply to Global Workforce Seasonal Visa or Peak Seasonal Visa applications. Job Change applications are also unaffected — the English check applies at the point of a new visa application, not when changing employer or role under an existing visa.[^1]

## What this means for your recruitment planning

The English evidence obligation sits with the applicant, not the employer. But a gap in evidence delays or ends the worker's visa application — and that falls back on your timeline and your hire.

If an applicant for a skill level 3 AEWV cannot demonstrate the required standard, INZ will decline their application. A job check token in hand — including one issued before 1 June 2026 — does not override the applicant-level visa requirement.[^1]

In practice:

- If you are recruiting internationally for skill level 3 roles, check whether candidates hold a qualifying test result, relevant citizenship, or an eligible qualification before committing to a Job Check.
- If you have existing workers at skill level 3 whose AEWVs expire after 1 December 2026, confirm whether they provided English evidence in a previous AEWV application. If they did, no further evidence is needed on renewal.
- For workers who have not previously demonstrated English and whose visas expire after December 2026, test booking and results windows vary by test type and provider. Check timelines with the relevant testing body and factor them into visa application planning before the renewal date is close.

## What to do

1. Identify which of your current AEWV workers hold skill level 3 roles and check whether their visas expire before or after 1 December 2026.
2. For workers whose visas expire after 1 December 2026, confirm whether they provided English evidence in a previous AEWV application. If they did, no further action is needed under this requirement.
3. For workers who have not previously demonstrated English and whose visas expire after December 2026, start the conversation now about which route they will use — test, citizenship history, or qualification.
4. For international recruitment into skill level 3 roles, add an English evidence check to your pre-Job-Check process alongside market-rate pay verification and NOL classification.
5. Check the current INZ page on AEWV English requirements to confirm the approved countries for the citizenship and qualification routes, as these lists are updated periodically.[^2]

## What we don't know

INZ has not published a breakdown of how many current skill level 3 AEWV holders have previously provided English evidence and would therefore be exempt on renewal. The proportion of level 3 workers who will face the requirement for the first time — and the spread across sectors — is not publicly available. It is also not yet clear whether INZ will issue further transitional guidance for workers who fall near the 1 December 2026 cut-off. If you have workers in that window, the most reliable path is to check directly with INZ or a licensed immigration adviser.
