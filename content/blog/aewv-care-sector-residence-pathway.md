The Care Workforce Work to Residence pathway gives qualifying care and support workers in New Zealand a route to resident visa status.[^1] What makes or breaks a worker's application is 24 months of continuous employment at the qualifying pay level — and it is the employer's records that prove that period was met. An accredited employer who has not been tracking the right evidence alongside standard payroll may find they cannot support a worker's application when the time comes, not because anything went wrong in principle, but because the record does not exist.

## Why this matters now

Many care sector employers first took on AEWV workers in 2023 and 2024, when the visa settings stabilised and demand for migrant care workers grew. Those employment relationships are now past the 12-month mark and approaching or reaching the 24-month qualifying window. Employers who have not connected their payroll and compliance records to the pathway's specific requirements may be approaching that window without the documentation to support an application. The second year is too late to start building the record from scratch.

## How the pathway works — the employer's role

The Care Workforce Work to Residence pathway requires a worker to hold continuous employment in a qualifying role for 24 months and to be paid at or above the level 4 rate under the pay equity settlement for support workers.[^1] The level 4 rate is $28.25 per hour as at the time of writing — this figure is subject to review under the support workers pay equity settlement process, and employers should verify the current rate before any application is assessed.[^1]

The worker applies for residence. The employer does not apply on their behalf. But the evidence the worker provides comes from the employer's records: payslips, timesheets, and employment agreements that demonstrate continuous employment at the qualifying pay rate across the full 24-month window. If those records are incomplete, the worker's application is weakened — and the gap is the employer's.

## What "continuous employment" means in practice

INZ's qualifying period condition requires continuous employment — not just that the worker was employed at some point during 24 months, but that the employment was unbroken and the qualifying pay rate was maintained throughout.[^1]

In practice this means tracking:

- The start date and any breaks, restructures, or changes in employment status during the period
- The pay rate for each pay period across the window, checked against the qualifying level
- Any changes to the employment agreement — whether the agreement was varied, a different role was taken on, or hours were changed — and whether those changes affected the pay level or continuity

A payroll system that runs accurately through the period produces most of this data. The gap is usually in presentation: whether the data can be assembled and read by a residence assessor who has not seen the employer's systems and is looking for a continuous, unbroken record.

## The accreditation connection

Active AEWV employer accreditation is a condition of the employment that contributes to the qualifying period. An employer whose accreditation lapses — through renewal failure, a compliance-driven stand-down, or administrative oversight — creates a gap in the qualifying record that is not cured by the worker's pay rate during that period.[^2]

This is the most common silent risk in the care sector residence pathway: an employer who has been paying correctly but allows accreditation to slip even briefly may inadvertently interrupt the continuity the worker's application requires. The [accreditation lapse article]({{< ref "/blog/aewv-accreditation-lapse-consequences" >}}) covers that scenario in full.

For care employers with a cohort of workers approaching the 24-month mark, accreditation renewal is not a compliance formality — it is part of the pathway support.

## What employers often miss

The pay equity rate is the most visible requirement because it is specific and measurable. Two further gaps appear more often:

**Hours evidence.** Workers whose hours fluctuate — seasonal increases, reduced contracted hours, or additional shifts — need payslips that reflect actual hours, not just the nominal contracted rate. A payroll process based on contracted hours without timesheet reconciliation may not show whether the qualifying hourly rate was maintained when hours changed. The [record-keeping article]({{< ref "/blog/aewv-record-keeping-audit-ready" >}}) covers the wage-and-time record standard that applies here.

**Agreement chain.** A worker who moved roles, changed departments, or received a variation to their employment agreement during the qualifying period needs a traceable chain of documents. A verbal rate change or a payroll update without a written amendment creates a gap between what the record shows and what actually happened. That gap is visible to a residence assessor.

## What to do

1. Identify which workers in your care workforce are on AEWV, have been employed for more than 12 months, and are employed under pay equity terms that qualify for the Care Workforce pathway. These are the workers approaching the 24-month mark.
2. For each, produce a pay rate check covering every pay period since their start date, comparing the actual hourly rate paid against the level 4 qualifying rate. Document any gaps and address them before the 24-month mark where they are resolvable.
3. Confirm your accreditation renewal date. If your current accreditation expires before any qualifying worker reaches 24 months, renewing on time is part of the pathway support — treat it as urgent.
4. Review your employment agreement records for workers in the qualifying cohort. If a variation was made without a written amendment, or if a role change was handled informally, address the documentation now.
5. Let workers in the qualifying cohort know what they will need from you and when. Workers who understand what their employer's records must show are better positioned to flag problems early and to engage a licensed immigration adviser at the right time.

## What we don't know

The Care Workforce Work to Residence pathway has been amended since its introduction, and the specific documentary requirements INZ assessors apply to residence applications are set out in the Operational Manual rather than the public-facing guidance pages. The $28.25 level 4 rate cited here should be verified against the current pay equity schedule before any application is prepared.

INZ has not published outcome data for care sector residence applications under this pathway. The pattern described here — a record gap being the common failure mode — is drawn from published guidance on what INZ assessors look for, not from disclosed case outcomes.
