AEWV MSD engagement is no longer an uploaded Engagement Check. Since 10 March 2025, for ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5 roles, the Job Check submission is a declaration that the employer engaged in good faith with Work and Income — and the underlying engagement records are what the declaration has to be defensible against.[^1] [^2] The compliance load did not disappear with the document upload. It shifted into the records you keep before the declaration goes in.

## Why this matters now

The Job Check is the gate every AEWV hire passes through, and the labour market test is the part of it most likely to draw scrutiny at a post-accreditation check (PAC) for roles at ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5.[^3] [^4] From 10 March 2025, INZ stopped requiring the MSD Engagement Check document at Job Check submission — but kept the obligation itself, recast as a declaration.[^1] A declaration without a record behind it is a finding waiting for an audit. The Immigration Employment Infringement Scheme, in force since 11 April 2024, gives that finding teeth it did not previously have.[^5]

## When MSD engagement actually applies

The trigger is the skill level of the role, not the visa class. INZ requires Work and Income engagement before applying for a Job Check where the role is at ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5.[^2] Roles at ANZSCO skill levels 1 to 3 — and roles on the National Occupation List (NOL) that can be used for an AEWV — do not require the engagement, though they remain subject to the general advertising obligation covered below.[^4]

Two 2026 reclassifications matter. From 9 March 2026, three occupations moved from skill level 3 to skill level 4 — pet groomer, nanny, and kennel hand — bringing them inside the MSD engagement and English-language obligations they were previously outside of.[^6] If you hire into any of those three, you are now inside this rule.

The Green List and the NZD $70.00 per hour rate carve roles out of the advertising obligation but do not carve them out of every Job Check requirement; check the specific role-side rules rather than assuming exemption.[^4]

## The 10 March 2025 change, restated plainly

Before 10 March 2025, employers attached an MSD Engagement Check document — issued by Work and Income — to the Job Check application. From 10 March 2025:

> If you apply for a job check on or after 10 March 2025, you no longer need to provide an Engagement Check from the Ministry of Social Development.

> When you apply for a job check, you must declare you have engaged in good faith with Work and Income.[^1]

The declaration is what INZ sees at submission. The records that prove the declaration is true are what an assessor sees at PAC. The shift is administrative, not substantive — and the practical effect for record keeping is to push more weight onto what the employer holds in their own files.

## What "good faith engagement" actually requires

INZ defines good faith as being:

> honest and genuine in trying to recruit any suitable New Zealand workers or residents who are available and can do the job.[^2]

INZ's page lists the minimum employer actions:

- complete Work and Income's vacancy form
- respond within 3 working days when Work and Income contacts you
- keep qualifications and job specifications consistent across all platforms you advertise on
- list the job (unless Work and Income states it has no suitable candidates)
- allow up to 10 working days for referrals plus time to interview
- interview suitable candidates Work and Income refers to you.[^2]

Work and Income adds two binding conditions of its own:

- the employer must engage directly — "you can't have an immigration professional (e.g. a Licensed Immigration Adviser or LIA) do it for you"
- listings run "usually around 10 working days", and Work and Income issues verification only where no suitable local candidates were found.[^7]

The "engage directly" rule is the one most often forgotten when an immigration adviser is running the Job Check end-to-end. The adviser can support and advise; the employer has to be the party on Work and Income's record.

## The advertising rule sits alongside, not inside, MSD engagement

The advertising requirement is a separate obligation that applies more broadly than the MSD engagement requirement. INZ's binding text:

> You must advertise a job for a minimum of: 14 calendar days for jobs at ANZSCO levels 1 to 3; 14 calendar days for jobs included on the list of NOL occupations that can be used for an AEWV or Specific Purpose Work Visa (AEWV), or 21 calendar days for jobs at ANZSCO levels 4 and 5.[^4]

No advertising is required if the position is on the Green List or pays at least NZD $70.00 per hour, and advertising is not required for a Global Workforce Seasonal Visa job check.[^4] The advertising window has to close within 90 days of the Job Check application or the evidence does not count.[^3]

The two obligations interact. For an ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5 role, the employer needs both: a 21-day advertising window on a relevant NZ-wide job-listing website, *and* MSD engagement that meets the good-faith standard above. Doing one does not satisfy the other.

## What the records actually have to show

INZ's engagement page names the evidence INZ expects to be held:

> a copy of the completed Work and Income form, including the date it was submitted, details of your business, title of the job available, the number of positions available, and ANZSCO code.[^2]

In practice, a defensible engagement pack for each Job Check at ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5 also contains:

- screenshots of the Work and Income vacancy listing while it was live, dated.
- the Work and Income reference number, the dates the listing opened and closed.
- the list of candidates referred by Work and Income, with the outcome of each — interviewed, offered, declined, and the reason.
- the parallel advertising evidence — screenshots of the role live on a NZ-wide jobsite, with dates that satisfy the 21-day rule and the 90-day-window rule.
- the employer's own contemporaneous note that the declaration submitted with the Job Check was supported by the above.

Work and Income's own page asks employers to "tell us the outcome. Let us know why candidates were not suitable or successful."[^7] A per-candidate decline log is the cleanest format that satisfies both that ask and the wider PAC standard.

*AEWV Compliance is scoping a tool in this area — a structured engagement pack per Job Check, with the declaration tied back to the records that defend it, is one of the deliverables we are testing demand for.*

## The recurring failure mode

The patterns at PAC for ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5 hires are predictable:

- **No engagement record because an adviser ran the Job Check.** The adviser holds notes; the employer's own file is empty. The "engage directly" rule from Work and Income then becomes a separate finding alongside the missing record.
- **Engagement done; outcome of each referred candidate undocumented.** Work and Income referred three candidates; the file shows no record of contact, interview, or decline reason. The good-faith standard is then unevidenced.
- **The 90-day advertising window expired before the Job Check.** Advertising closed in March, the Job Check went in late June, the file holds no live advertising during the window — the advertising leg fails on a timing rule, not a content rule.

The pattern is uniform: the obligation was met; the record of meeting it was not built at the time. The [Job Check declines note]({{< ref "/blog/aewv-job-check-declined-reasons" >}}) covers the wider evidence-pack shape this post does not.

## What to do this week

1. For every Job Check submitted in the past 12 months at ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5, locate the engagement pack — Work and Income form copy, screenshots, candidate outcomes, parallel advertising. Note any gap by Job Check.
2. Confirm the employer of record on the Work and Income vacancy listing is the employer, not the immigration adviser. If an adviser ran it, build the corrective record now and review the process for future Job Checks.
3. Run the 21-day-versus-14-day check against your standard advertising practice. If the default is 14 days for everything, ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5 roles are under-advertised on a rule, not a judgement.
4. Build a standing template for the per-candidate decline log — name, date referred, contact attempted, outcome, reason — and apply it from the next Job Check forward. Retrofit prior Job Checks honestly where you can evidence the underlying decisions.
5. Brief whoever runs Job Checks — internal hiring lead, HR business partner, retained adviser — on the engage-directly rule and on the 9 March 2026 reclassification of pet groomer, nanny, and kennel hand into skill level 4. Roles that did not require engagement in February now do.

The advertising and market-rate reasoning sit inside the wider Job Check evidence pack; the [Job Check declines note]({{< ref "/blog/aewv-job-check-declined-reasons" >}}) and the [audit-ready record keeping note]({{< ref "/blog/aewv-record-keeping-audit-ready" >}}) both cover ground this post deliberately stays narrow on.

## What we don't know

- INZ does not publish a finding-rate breakdown for engagement-record gaps versus advertising-record gaps at PAC. The patterns flagged here are drawn from published guidance and accounts shared with us; the underlying distribution is not public.
- The status of the engagement obligation for NOL occupations that map onto historical ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5 roles is set by INZ's NOL occupation list rather than by ANZSCO directly; check the role-specific page if your hire falls into a recently reclassified occupation.[^6] [^8]
- Work and Income's published cadence ("usually around 10 working days") leaves room for variance in practice. If a listing closes early or runs long because of Work and Income's own timing, hold the dated correspondence — your record needs to explain the deviation, not assert a standard timetable.
