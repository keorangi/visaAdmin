The INZ fee schedule for AEWV employer accreditation is public and fixed. Standard accreditation costs $775, high-volume costs $1,280, and each Job Check costs $735.[^1] What those fees cover is narrower than most employers expect: they pay for INZ's cost of processing the application. They do not cover the adviser fees, employment agreement preparation, recruitment costs, or internal HR time that typically make up the larger part of the total cost of an AEWV hire. Understanding what is and is not in the fee helps employers build accurate budgets and make better decisions about where to invest in the process.

## Why this matters now

With more than 29,000 accredited employers in New Zealand,[^2] many businesses are now in their second or third accreditation cycle and building multi-year AEWV hiring programmes. At that scale, the gap between the published fee and the actual per-hire cost compounds. An employer who has budgeted on the INZ fee schedule alone will consistently underestimate what each AEWV worker costs to hire and retain — and will find it harder to justify the programme internally when the real costs land.

The fees are set by regulation. The schedule in this article reflects the INZ fee page as accessed in June 2026.[^1] Fees can and do change between reviews; confirm the current schedule at the INZ source before using these figures for planning.

## The INZ fee schedule in full

All fees are GST-inclusive.[^1]

| Application type | Fee |
|---|---|
| Standard accreditation | $775 |
| High-volume accreditation | $1,280 |
| Triangular / controlling-third-party accreditation | $4,060 |
| Upgrade: standard to high-volume | $505 |
| Job Check | $735 |
| Reconsideration of a declined application | $250 |

Fees are payable when you apply, not when a decision is made. INZ does not refund accreditation or Job Check fees if an application is declined. If you seek reconsideration of a declined decision, the $250 reconsideration fee applies as a separate charge. There is no separate fee for renewal; renewal applications are assessed under the same fee schedule as new applications.[^1]

The [accreditation types article]({{< ref "/blog/aewv-accreditation-types-which-to-choose" >}}) explains the differences between standard, high-volume, and triangular accreditation if you need to confirm which fee applies to your organisation.

## What the INZ fee covers

The INZ fee covers the cost of INZ processing your application. That is the assessor time, the administrative workflow, and the INZ systems involved in evaluating and recording the accreditation or Job Check decision.[^1]

It does not extend beyond INZ's own processing costs. In particular, the fee does not cover:

**Immigration adviser fees.** Most employers use a licensed immigration adviser (RMA) to prepare and submit accreditation, Job Check, and worker visa applications. Adviser fees vary significantly by firm and by the complexity of the application. They are not published in a standardised way and are not captured in the INZ fee.

**Employment agreement preparation.** AEWV workers must be employed under written employment agreements meeting NZ employment law requirements. For roles where a tested template does not already exist, legal review or drafting of the agreement adds to the cost of the hire.

**Recruitment advertising.** Most Job Checks require the employer to demonstrate genuine efforts to hire a New Zealander, including a minimum 14-day advertising period.[^3] Platform fees, agency fees, and the staff time managing the advertising and applicant-screening process are costs external to the INZ fee. The [genuine efforts article]({{< ref "/blog/aewv-genuine-efforts-hire-new-zealander" >}}) covers what the advertising requirement produces and what records it generates.

**Internal HR and management time.** Completing an accreditation application, producing supporting documents, running the Job Check advertising process, delivering the settlement information obligation, and maintaining records ready for a post-accreditation check all carry staff time that is not reflected in the INZ fee. For smaller employers without dedicated HR, this is often the largest single cost of the programme.

## The per-hire cost: where the scale effects are

The accreditation fee is a one-time cost per accreditation period; the Job Check fee recurs per role. For a standard accredited employer running one hire cycle over two years, the INZ cost floor is $775 (accreditation) + $735 (Job Check) = $1,510 before any other cost. For an employer running multiple Job Checks a year, the Job Check fee scales with volume.

The economics shift significantly by accreditation type. A triangular or controlling-third-party employer pays $4,060 for accreditation, plus $735 per Job Check for each placement. At low placement volumes, the per-hire cost of accreditation alone is significant. At higher volumes, the fixed accreditation cost amortises and the Job Check fee becomes the dominant variable.

## What to do

1. Check the INZ fees page directly[^1] before finalising any budget for accreditation or Job Checks. Fees are set by regulation and may have been updated since this article was published.
2. If you are preparing a business case for AEWV hiring, structure the budget into three separate lines: INZ fees, adviser and legal fees, and internal time. Blending them makes it harder to understand where efficiency gains are possible.
3. For repeat Job Checks on similar roles, check whether a tested employment agreement template and a documented advertising process can reduce the marginal cost of subsequent hires. The first hire in any role type carries the highest setup cost.
4. If you use an adviser, confirm whether their fee covers both the Job Check and the worker's visa application, or whether the visa application is a separate engagement. The two processes often run in parallel but are billed differently across firms.
5. Track internal staff time per hire for at least the first two or three AEWV hires. The data supports internal cost-per-hire reporting and helps identify which parts of the process to streamline.

## What we don't know

INZ does not publish aggregate data on what accredited employers spend with advisers, or on the legal and HR costs associated with AEWV hiring. The total cost-per-hire figure that would be most useful for employers building business cases — a realistic range covering INZ fees plus the common add-ons — is not available from any published source.
