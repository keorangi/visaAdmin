Controlling third party accreditation carries a workforce composition condition. At least 15% of the workers you place in triangular employment arrangements must be New Zealand citizens or residents, each guaranteed at least 30 paid hours a week.[^1] Immigration New Zealand checks this at post-accreditation checks.[^2] The condition does not apply to standard or high-volume accreditation. And INZ does not prescribe the calculation method.

## Why this matters now

INZ's PAC guide names the 15% condition as an evidence category for controlling third party and franchisee accreditation holders.[^2] The [audit-ready record-keeping note]({{< ref "/blog/aewv-record-keeping-audit-ready" >}}) covers the broader PAC evidence picture; this post focuses on the condition itself — what it requires, who is in scope, and where the published guidance runs thin.

## What the condition requires

INZ's accreditation type guidance states the condition in plain terms: at least 15% of staff placed must be New Zealand citizens or residents and be guaranteed at least 30 hours of work each week.[^1]

The INZ Operational Manual, chapter WA2.20, adds specificity:[^3]

> A minimum of 15% of the employer's employees who are placed in triangular employment arrangements must be New Zealand citizens or residents who are guaranteed at least 30 paid hours per week.

Two things to note from the Manual wording.

**"Guaranteed at least 30 paid hours" is a contractual minimum, not an average.** The worker must have a contractual floor of 30 paid hours per week. Hours actually worked across a pay period do not substitute for a written guarantee. A casual or variable-hours arrangement is unlikely to satisfy this unless the employment agreement sets that floor explicitly.

**The base for the 15% is workers placed in triangular arrangements, not the whole workforce.** The Manual uses "employees who are placed in triangular employment arrangements" — not all employees. Workers on other arrangements sit outside the denominator.

## Who the condition applies to — and who it does not

The 15% condition applies to:[^1] [^2] [^3]

- **Controlling third party accreditation holders.** This is the accreditation type for labour-hire operators and employers who place workers at client sites under triangular arrangements. The condition is ongoing — it does not drop away once you hold accreditation.
- **Franchisee accreditation holders (historical).** Franchisee accreditation closed to new applications on 16 June 2024.[^4] Existing holders carrying this type into renewal may face an earlier version of the condition; check the current renewal terms.

The condition does **not** apply to standard accreditation (up to five AEWV holders) or high-volume accreditation (six or more). If you hold either of those types, this post does not cover your obligations.

### The construction exception

For employers placing workers in construction occupations, the threshold is higher: **35%, not 15%**.[^3] This exception appears in the Operational Manual but not on INZ's public employer guidance pages. An employer placing construction workers under a triangular arrangement who reads only the public guidance will find the wrong number.

### The single-placement exemption

If you are placing only one employee in a triangular employment arrangement, the NZ-worker condition does not apply at all.[^3] This exemption is in the Manual but absent from public pages. It is likely to cover sole-trader or very small labour-hire operations.

## The calculation problem

Here is where the published guidance runs thin. INZ's public pages describe the condition using "staff placed" without specifying whether this means headcount or full-time equivalent. The Operational Manual uses "employees" in the same way, without defining the calculation base.[^3]

The practical implication is that an employer with a PAC approaching has to make a defensible judgment call — headcount at a point in time, an average over a period, or FTE — and then apply it consistently. No source confirms which INZ assessors use.

What can be said with confidence: whatever method you use, you need to be able to produce the underlying data — the payroll or placement record that generated the number — within the window an assessor gives you. An assertion that "we're around 20%" without a supporting calculation is not evidence.

*AEWV Compliance is scoping a tool in this area — producing the 15% calculation from payroll data on demand is one of the deliverables we are testing demand for.*

## What to do this week

1. Confirm which accreditation type you hold. If it is standard or high-volume, this post does not apply to your obligations.
2. If you hold controlling third party accreditation, identify all workers currently placed in triangular arrangements and note their citizenship or residency status and contracted weekly hours.
3. Run the 15% calculation using the most conservative plausible method — headcount, point-in-time. Note whether you meet the threshold and by how much margin, and keep the underlying data.
4. Check that each qualifying NZ citizen or resident has a contractual guarantee of 30 paid hours per week in their employment agreement — not just a typical pattern or an average.
5. Decide on a calculation cadence (quarterly, six-monthly) and name the person responsible. The condition is ongoing and will be checked at PAC.

For the broader PAC context, the [post-accreditation check walkthrough]({{< ref "/blog/post-accreditation-check-what-to-expect" >}}) covers how assessors approach each evidence category.

## What we don't know

- INZ does not publicly specify whether the 15% is calculated by headcount or FTE, or over what reference period. The Manual confirms the condition but does not resolve this. We do not know how INZ assessors treat this ambiguity at PAC.
- The INZ Operational Manual chapter WA2.20 is dated November 2023 and marked archived. A current replacement may exist under a different chapter number. Before relying on the construction exception or single-placement exemption, verify the current Manual via the TOC at immigration.govt.nz/opsmanual/toc.htm.
- The precise evidence format INZ expects at PAC for this condition is not published. The PAC guide says "proof" and "records" without prescribing a template.
